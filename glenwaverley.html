Day 9: Glen Waverley

December 31, 2013
Scroll Down

Slept in, much to Dad’s annoyance. Walked up to the shops. Saw lots of really nice restaurants mainly Asian.

Went to Norton Park for lunch which was just up the road. Had a walk around the park. Stopped in at the shops on the way back and bought some more supplies - tried to find a jacket for Mum as we were planning to see the Penguin Parade at Phillip Island and people kept telling us it would be cold. Mum ended up putting a car blanket around herself.

Saw some Cape Barren geese. PI much bigger than we thought it was. Saw the PI race track which was much smaller than we thought it was. PI was bigger and the racetrack smaller.

Arrived in good time. Upgraded to the VIP viewing area because the lady said that the other section had 3,500 people the other night. Our section would only have 190 max. Went down and waited until sunset. We were in the second row but got to move down to the front row when the ranger group was smaller than expected.

Went and got some food while we waited until sunset.

Penguins started to come out of the water. There were heaps of them. They were walking straight past us, some of them walking up the hill. Some of them have burrows up to 2 and a half kilometres away. A baby came out and was trying to get food from all the passing adults but was rejected every time.

Some penguins were mating and others were arguing. Some of the penguins had metal tags on their wings - this indicated that they were at least 11 or 12 years old as the practise is no longer in use. Penguins live about 7 or 8 years although one lived until he was about 26. Some of the penguins walked up to the car park and we had to look under the car before we left. We could see the penguins from when we left our seats until we went into the main building.

The rangers even stopped people while one of the penguins walked across the path.

Mum bought me a penguin which we later christened Phillip Monet.

Drove back to Glen Waverley. Arrived about 10 minutes after midnight. Saw some fireworks on the way home.
