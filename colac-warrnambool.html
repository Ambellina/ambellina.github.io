Day 7: Colac -> Warrnambool

December 29, 2013
Scroll Down

We left at about 8.30 a.m. and went on a different route to Apollo Bay. Stopped at a little place just outside Colac for a wee and found a snake. Went for a slide down the slippery dip.

Drove on further and Dad commented on how good the road was. This was before we turned off onto many miles of narrow, windy road through the rainforest. Stopped so Mum could have another wee in front of everyone driving past. Fortunately there was nobody. I drove magnificently as acknowledged by Dad. Joined up with the road from yesterday. Stopped just before Apollo Bay to admire the view and take some photos.

Arrived at Apollo Bay. Went for a drive through the main street and had a look at the harbour. Dad walked a bit further and had a look at the beach. Stopped and had cheese and bacon rolls from the ridiculously busy bakery. Ate them in the park. Got a real estate magazine to look at the prices.

Travelled on to the Great Otway National Park and decided to have a look at the Cape Otway Light Station. Saw a sign that had been altered to make the animals look pre-historic. Took a photo on the way back. Saw some cows wandering along the road. Also saw some koalas and stopped to take photos on the way back.

Me and Dad visited the Light Station while Mum stayed behind. We saw some of the Cape Otway Signal Station and learned about the first cable that was laid to Tasmania and the guide told a story about how it took 28 minutes for news to reach Australia from England after being transferred via multiple morse code stations. We saw the museum and handled a dinosaur egg.

Climbed up the lighthouse and walked around up the top. The lighthouse was built in 1847-48 and was the first one in mainland Australia1. Took some pictures. It was very windy. Went and looked at the bunker and the whales (one carved out of the wood, the other made of stones and metal). Stuck our head in the café but they didn’t have any ice blocks.

When we got back to the entrance we saw Mum buying an ice block to cool down.

On the way out we took some photos of the koalas. One was very active and was crawling around and eating.

We travelled on to a couple of lookouts. At one Dad insulted me by telling everyone that I took too many selfies when I’d only taken about 3 all trip.

Stopped at the Gibson Steps. Lots of buses and people.

Arrived at the Twelve Apostles. Mum managed to get out of the car and walk to the start of the Twelve Apostles. She didn’t wait for me to take any photos of her before she went back to the shop2. Mum said she decided to slowly head back up the hill as more and more people were arriving. She sat and waited for Dad and me to wander around and take photos.

Met George Ellis, the conductor from the AYC Alumni events!

Expertly took photos for a few other people.

Hundreds of people and cars.

Helicopters flying around.

Mum was astounded at the number of buses, helicopters and people. She said that when she and her friend Carol were there in 1974 it was just them.

Loch Ard Gorge – saw thunder cave – 500 metres but we underestimated HOW FAR THAT WAS.

Drove back to the Gorge and I had a tantrum again and didn’t want to come. But eventually changed my mind and it was almost as good as the T A. One big Apostle.3

Visited Port Campbell. Had a look around the town. Took some photos from the lookout.

Went to the Archway, then London Bridge.

Saw the Bay of Islands and the shipwreck.

Drove on to Warrnambool. Checked in. The motel was very clean. The proprietor was telling Dad how bad the traffic had been on the Great Ocean road yesterday.

Dad had fluff on his beard when he ordered pizza. I accidentally set off the car alarm.

Drove round to the breakwater and the lookout.

Watched the darts on Foxtel.

1 - It took approx. 70 men, 10 months to build the lighthouse - this involved cutting rock from the cliffs at Parker River Inlet several kms away, carving the stones to exact sizes and carting them using bullock teams.
2 - I got a photo of her back at least. The photo of her front was taken on her camera, and must have been taken by Dad I guess, or a friendly stranger.
3 - It took me a while to make sense of this section and reconcile it with the photos and map. I believe it refers to us viewing ‘The Island Archway’ (which isn’t, in fact, an archway since it fell in 2009) and The Razorback - and that I ended up liking them almost as much as the Twelve Apostles.
