Day 3: Merimbula -> Traralgon

December 25, 2013
Scroll Down

Merry Christmas!

Up at 7, had to eat breakfast from coffee cups as there were no plates.

No exciting border crossing, missed the sign!1

Morning tea at Cann River. Rang up Nana and Poppy. Helped some people with directions.

I won lots of games of animal spotting.

Drove on to Lakes Entrance which was a bit of a dive. Found it difficult to get a nice spot in the shade, but eventually got one. Had our chicken sandwiches and watermelon. Walked across to check out the beach briefly. Lots of ships.

Drop bear.2

Passed through a nice town – Rosedale – Saw statue of Patrobas who won the Melbourne Cup in 1915.3

Arrived in Traralgon. Didn’t like the look of the motel but it was really quite nice on the inside and the owners had even wrapped a present for us! Ferrero Chocs.

Drove around town to try and find something for dinner – lots of shops, but only Maccas was open, devastating! Drove to Glengarry (didn’t quite make it to Toongabbie) to look for more places, no luck, and nearly hit a Falcon (the bird, not the car). Then tried Morwell which was also unlucky on the food, but we found a beautiful rose garden and got out to take some pictures. It was really hot so we tried to be quick. Got a few really nice shots.

Back to Traralgon and got Maccas – Dad got a salad, I got a wrap and Mum had some Angus.

Had a game of cards and I’m the President.

Forgot to eat my pudding.

1 - Not sure what sign we were referring to here, as I clearly have a photo of the Victoria sign.
2 - Who knows what this was in reference to?
3 - The Patrobas Story is the true story of the only Gippsland horse to win a Melbourne cup.
This event took place in 1915, in the same year Patrobas also won the Caulfield Guineas and the Derby. This feat has not only not been repeated but is unlikely to be.
Patrobas was owned by a local lady Mrs Edith Widdis, who resided at Nambrok House, the current home of Mr Peter McGauran MHR. Mrs Widdis received Patrobas as a birthday present from her husband and after being beaten in a two horse race at the now defunct Rosedale Racecourse, Patrobas was sent to Mr Charlie Wheeler a significant trainer of the day. The result of this action is now in the annals of racing history. Mrs Widdis became the first female owner of the Melbourne Cup. The rider Bobby Lewis was the first race rider to ride four Melbourne Cup winners. Patrobas carried the rose pink silks belonging to Mrs Widdis and saddle cloth number 19.
The Patrobas statue was erected as a result of the dedication of the Rosedale Chamber of Commerce and Industry and is constructed from plate steel. The statue was unveiled and dedicated by Mr Peter Hall MLC on November 4th 2007. The cup won by Patrobas is on display at the Arts Centre in Sale.
