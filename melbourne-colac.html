Day 6: Melbourne -> Colac

December 28, 2013
Scroll Down

Today we leave Melbourne to travel along the Great Ocean Road to Colac via Apollo Bay. After breakfast Dad and I took some of the bags to the car and then walked to the casino. We passed some English yobbos who really thought that they were going to win the 4th Test. Poor fools.1 I got us lost trying to get to the casino by walking through the shops. The casino was very big and impressive. It had a nice Christmas tree in the foyer. There were a few people playing roulette and card games but not as many as you would find in the Sydney casino. There were a lot of poker machines and I put $5 in one (Phantom) but we only got two small payouts. Pretty pitiful.

On the way to the casino Dad thought that he had lost the key to the unit. He had a hole in his pocket and thought the key had fallen through. I had visions of having to retrace our steps until Dad found the key in one of his other pockets.

On the way back we walked through Desgraves Lane. I told Dad that I thought that he had fallen down the gutter, but he hadn’t. But I put a curse on him so that he fell down the gutter at the next corner.

Dad took the rest of the cases to the car and was going to then come and pick Mum and me up from in front of the building. Dad rang me to say that he couldn’t find the ticket for the car park. Couldn’t find his arse in the bath I thought. But Dad rang the people who managed the car park and everything was soon sorted.

On the way to Geelong we were delayed by a car accident. Traffic was just crawling along. We were delayed again going into Geelong for some reason that we couldn’t explain. Geelong was o.k. but not as good as Ballarat. We had morning tea at Geelong near the ground of the Geelong Cats.

We drove on to Torquay. Pitiful bumper to bumper traffic again for mile after mile. Torquay was a lovely place but very busy with hardly a parking space anywhere. We had a look at some of the beaches and looked for Bell’s Beach which we eventually found. Only very small waves.

We bought some petrol. We decided to drive to Anglesea for lunch. We had fish and chips again. When we went into the shop to order lunch it was about 39 degrees but while we were in there a cool blustery change arrived and the temperate dropped about 15 degrees in minutes.

We were going to have lunch at one of the Anglesea beaches at one of the tables but had to finally have it in the car because it was blowing so much and there were too many flies.

The drive to Lorne was again bumper to bumper. Fortunately the drive from Lorne to Apollo Bay was much better. Every time we stopped on the Great Ocean Road we were nearly blown away. The views were great however.

We decided not to visit Apollo Bay and to turn off just before the town and travel on to our accommodation at Colac. We arrived at about 20 to 7. On the way there were a lot of branches and limbs on the road. One limb fell just in front of the car and punctured a hole in the front of the Jag. Svetty was very upset. We stopped on the way for Mum and me to go the toilet. There was a big poo on the floor.

While Dad was booking in there was another man booking in who was talking about how bad the traffic had been on the Great Ocean Road.

We had a quick drive around Colac. Dad bought seafood noodles for Mum for tea and some prawn chips. Me and Dad just had vegemite muffins and coke. I also had an apple. We rang Nana and Poppy to see how they were going.

Mum wasn’t impressed with Colac.

1 - Australia won by 8 wickets and they went on to win the series 5-0.
