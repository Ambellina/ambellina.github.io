Day 5: Melbourne

December 27, 2013
Scroll Down

I didn’t sleep well because of the roll-out bed that had a hard bar in the middle1. I had a shower and went out and bought some washing powder and some butter, vegemite and buns. Dad and I went for a walk while mum did some washing. Took Mum a while to figure out the washing machine / drier. Clothes were still damp the next morning. Mum did 4 or 5 loads.

Dad and I walked over the Yarra to the Art Gallery but it wasn’t open. We walked for miles through the gardens with their beautiful lawns and flowers and trees. We saw the Myer Music Bowl. They were just packing up after Carols by Candlelight. We visited the war memorial. As we were there at 10.00 o’clock we heard the last post and saw the light passing over the centre stone in the memorial. We also heard a brief lecture from the curator. We went upstairs and had a wonderful view of the city, of the Westgate Bridge and out to St Kilda. We visited the Botanical Gardens Visitors Centre. Dad was a stinge arse and wouldn’t buy me a reindeer even though it was half price. We walked through the Botanical Gardens and saw where they were going to do Wind in the Willows for the kids. Dad thought that Pooh Bear was in Wind in the Willows. We walked past Rod Laver Arena (there were lots of outside courts for the minor games) and the MCG where they were playing the second day of the 4th Test. Some of the people walking to the cricket were dressed in costumes. We saw statutes of Bill Ponsford and Dennis Lillee.

On the way back to the hotel Dad bought baguettes for lunch. He did this in French of course. They were delicious, except for the salami one that had pickles on it which I didn’t like very much.

Dad went to the carpark to pick up the car. I was sure that he would get lost even though he has a marvellous sense of direction and we would win the Amazing Race. He picked me and Mum up outside the hotel.

We drove to Ballarat and saw all the old buildings. We had a tram ride near the lake in a tram that was built in 1915.

On the way back we visited the Kryal Castle just to have a look from the outside. We looked for the native gardens as well but after we drove up this big hill around the winding road we couldn’t find them but we did get a nice view of Ballarat and the countryside. We saw a guy get pulled up by the police after the police whizzed past us at a million miles an hour. We were going to have a ride on the free tram around Melbourne but when we got back we saw that the tram was packed with people squeezed in like sardines.

We had a game of cards which I won easily.

Still didn’t have any pudding. Help it’s going to be 38 tomorrow!

1 - Judging by this photo, I must have given up and moved to the couch instead?
